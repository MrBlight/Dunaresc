## Rečnikul Dunaresc — Core Vocabulary
A starter dictionary of the most essential words in Dunaresc, each tagged with its source language. The Cro Croatian layer dominates everyday objects, nature, and emotion — the warm, immediate words. The Rom Romanian layer carries verbs, particles, and the grammar spine — the connective tissue. The Ger German layer handles built things, institutions, and abstractions — anything precise or man-made. Lat Latin is reserved for the high register: ceremony, law, religion. Every entry is shown in the new Croatian + German orthography.

| Dunaresc | English | Source | Notes |
|----------|---------|--------|-------|
| apa | water | Rom | Def. apa (same — Rom. feminine). |
| kruh | bread | Cro | Prestige Croatian retention. Never *pâine. |
| vin | wine | Rom | Identical in Lat/Rom — no source clash. |
| foc | fire | Rom | From Lat. focus. |
| sare | salt | Rom | Def. sarea. |
| haus | house (lit. dwelling) | Ger | Loan: Haus, kept capitalized in writing. |
| kuća | house (colloquial) | Cro | Warmer, Popularu form. "Sunt la kuća." |
| dom | home / homeland | Cro Lat | Shared root. National anthem core word. |
| Wagen | vehicle / car | Ger | Loan, always capitalized. |
| Werk | work / workshop | Ger | Loan; productive in compounds (Schul-Werk, Kode-Werk). |
| buch | book | Ger | Capitalized when in compounds (Kloud-Buch). |
| karte | card / map | Ger | From Karte; lowercased after assimilation. |
| telefon | telephone | Rom | International root, Romanian morphology. |
| masa | table | Rom | Def. masa. |
| stolica | chair | Cro | Replaces Rom. scaun in Popularu register. |

A note on completeness: a real Dunaresc dictionary would run to thousands of entries, with regional variants, archaic forms, and the productive compound system. This is the founders' kernel — the ~150 words that the language cannot do without, and the rule-system that generates the rest.

## Slang, Idioms & Proverbs
These are the phrases that make Danubian feel lived-in. Many are impossible to translate literally — they emerge from the specific collision of Romanian pastoral life, German precision culture, and Croatian directness. These are the things you'd hear in a Dunaresc household, not in any textbook.

### Proverbs (Zicale Dunarešti)
| Danubian | Literal | Actual meaning |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| Nu toci Wagen-ii merg brzo. | "Not all cars go quickly." | Don't judge someone before you see them work. (Romanian wisdom, German noun) |
| Lupul štie šuma lui. | "The wolf knows his forest." | Everyone is dangerous in their own territory. Don't start fights at home. |
| Vinul vechi nu are nevoie de Werk-ul nou. | "Old wine needs no new workshop." | Experience speaks for itself — don't over-explain your credentials. |
| Daca raul hladna este, peštele bajo inot. | "If the river is cold, the fish swims below." | Hard times push smart people underground. Lay low when things are rough. |
| Frumu vorbesc Latinei; oamenii reali vorbesc gut. | "The fancy speak Latin; real people speak well." | High registers impress no one if you have nothing real to say. |
| Mai bun un lup in šuma decat doi judex in Stadt. | "Better one wolf in the forest than two judges in the city." | Clear danger beats bureaucratic chaos. At least a wolf is honest. |

### Street Idioms (Frazeologie Popularu)
| Phrase | Gloss | Usage |
|--------|-------|-------|
| A dat Wagenul pe šuma. | "He traded the car for the forest." | He gave up something valuable for something wild and unpredictable. Impulsive life choices. |
| Vorbešt-o gut, cap de Stein! | "Say it well, stone-head!" | Get to the point. Used when someone rambles. Stein = German "stone." |
| E ca kruhul uscat. | "It's like dry bread." | Something boring, dry, or joyless. "That lecture was kruh uscat." |
| A intrat in Werkul mare. | "He entered the great Workshop." | He got into serious trouble — or, ironically, he finally got a real job. |
| Sa-ci fie Gott cu tine. | "May God be with you." (mixed Ger./Rom.) | Sincere farewell — but also used sarcastically when someone is about to do something stupid. |
| Nu face Lärm fara Werk. | "Don't make noise without work." (Ger. Lärm = noise) | Don't talk big without producing results. Anti-hype proverb. |

## Technical Neologisms
Modern Danubian — especially as spoken in the border towns and river settlements — has developed a rich layer of neologisms for digital life, high school culture, and Gen Z social dynamics. The language absorbs these through three strategies: German compounding (two roots, hyphen, capitalized), Romanian suffixation (-are for actions, -ist for people), and Croatian phonetic adoption (foreign words given Croatian plural -i).

| Danubian | Formation | Meaning |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Netz-postare | GerNetz + Rom. postare | A social media post. "Am facut o Netz-postare." |
| Bilduri | GerBild (image) + Cro. pl. -uri | Photos/pics. "Trimite-mi Bilduri!" = Send me pics! |
| Folgator / Folgatoare | Gerfolgen (to follow) + Rom. -ator | A follower (social media). Pl. Folgatori. |
| Svikeist | NewSwipe + -ist (agent) | Someone who mindlessly scrolls. Slightly derogatory. |
| Netz-Werk | GerNetz + Werk | The internet infrastructure; "going online." Distinct from just Netz (the web). |
| Gluma-virala | Romgluma (joke) + virala (viral, f.) | A viral meme or joke. "Asta e o gluma-virala buna." |
| Postareala | Romposta + augmentative -reala | Excessive posting, oversharing. "Nu mai face postareala!" |

# Expresii Tari · Strong Expressions
**Created by:** MrBlight | **Collaboration:** NaniIsNano

## Exclamations, Swears & Emotional Language
A language that can't curse isn't ready to be spoken. Danubian's exclamations draw on all four source languages — the frustration words tend to be Croatian (short, hard consonants), the awe words tend to be Latin (weight of history), and the affectionate insults tend to mix German precision nouns with Romanian diminutive warmth.

### Frustration & Anger
- `Ku' Lärm!` — lit. "What noise!" — General frustration. "What the hell!" (Ger)
- `Draga Veritas!` — lit. "Dear Truth!" — Exasperated disbelief. The Latin Veritas used like taking God's name. (Lat)
- `Šta je ovo?!` — lit. "What is this?!" — Pure Croatian frustration. Very natural in Popularu. (Cro)
- `Lua-te-ar Dunarea!` — lit. "May the Danube take you!" — Danubian-specific mild curse. Wishing someone would get swept away. (Rom)
- `Cap de Stein!` — lit. "Stone-head!" — Calling someone an idiot. German noun, Romanian structure. (Ger)
- `Merde-Werk!` — lit. "Shit-work!" — A total mess or useless project. French merde adopted into the compound. Considered mild. (New)

### Excitement & Awe
- `Magnus Gott!` — lit. "Great God!" — The strongest awe exclamation in Inalt register. Heard at ceremonies. (Lat Ger)
- `Jako bun!` — lit. "Very good!" (Croatian + Romanian) — Casual high-five language. Sports goals, good food. (Cro)
- `Pronto, Wagen!` — lit. "Ready, car!" — Excitement before doing something fast or exciting. Pre-race energy. (Lat Ger)
- `Živi Dunarea!` — lit. "Long live the Danube!" — National pride cry. Used at games, parades, moments of victory. (Cro Rom)

### Affectionate Insults (Insulte cu Caldura)
- `Lup-mic` — lit. "Little wolf." — Fond nickname for a clever, sly friend. High compliment. (Rom)
- `Kleiner Gradist` — lit. "Little tryhard." (Ger. klein + slang Gradist) — Affectionate ribbing of someone who works too hard. (Ger)
- `Prostul meu` — lit. "My little idiot." — Romanian term of absolute endearment. You'd only say this to your best friend. (Rom)
